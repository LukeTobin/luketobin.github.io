Natural Selection

Concept

An island survival game based around emergent gameplay. Play as a stranded individual who must survive in a harsh procedurally generated island using what limited resources they have available to them. Actions weigh more in this survival experience with a limited set of resources, a player must make bold decisions in what they want to use. Use your limited supply of wood for hunting? warmth? make unique compromises each run and experience an emergent world unravel. With each level being procedurally generated, a unique new scenario awaits you each time you play.

The game was developed as a university assignment based on the theme of emergent gameplay. It was the first large scale unity project I worked on and took a programming lead position in.

Notable Work

Procedural Generation

  • Pseudo-procedural level generation
  • Designer built levels

The procedural generation of Natural Selection was implemented in a way in which designers have impact over the generation. Heavy use of inspector values for editing generation properties and pre-built rooms which would be knit together allowed for us to have control over what would be created.

The generator would set up and connect a series of rooms together. These rooms would be pre-designed by designers, and altered and shaken up on each run, to not make all rooms almost identical to what they had previously.
