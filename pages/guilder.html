Guilder

Concept

Tactical Turn Based Roguelite with heavy emphasis on guild building. Recruit and control your group of heroes as you try reach the end. Synergize, and conquer the foes within your path, unlock new heroes and epic artifacts and make every single one of your runs fun and unique.

Development for this project lasted 16 weeks over spring. We used the scrum development methodology & github for version control.

Notable Work

Concepting

The concept for the game stemmed from market research into popular indie game titles. We quickly discovered an overlap between two markets with great indie success. Roguelike & Turn based, we decided to try capitalize on the gap in the market, by creating a unique game with key elements from both genres.

Our core design pillars were an engaging turn based gameplay, flexibility and composition building and meaningful decision making.

The gameplay loops consisted within the game would be similar to that of roguelike deckbuilders, taking inspiration for the genres new star games. Building a team composition and strategizing and utilizing your units effectively

Gameplay loops

The core gameplay loop would consist of 'Traversing a generated map' -> 'Battling with your units' -> 'Collect rewards and Recruit new units'

Units

  • Modular
  • Designer Friendly
  • Handled Combat, movement and AI

The unit system was set up with modularity & designers in mind. With unit's typically only differing based on ability usage, unit's should be able to be created at anytime by designers. This system allows for designers to enter relevant information and fill in the stats, abilities and behaviors of a unit.

Editing details, quick balance changes and prototyping was able to be done in rapid

succession with our framework.

This framework handled unit behaviors such as moving, attacking, pathfinding and enemy AI. The modularity of the system made interacting with the objects in our code simple and effective.
